Nineveh's North Palace

This palace was a triumph project after the victories over Babylon in 648 and Elam in 647. The stones were brought on Elamite chariots, and captured Arab kings were forced to do at least some of the work. The campaigns depicted in this building take place no later than 643 BC, so the palace will probably be completed in that year. According to Ashurbanipal, the old North Palace was a palace that Sennacherib had built and inhabited as crown prince and as king, and where Esarhaddon had grown up. However, this building had become dilapidated and Assurbanipal decided to build his palace there.

The palace was not far below the surface of the hill, and much of the palace has been destroyed by fire, erosion and later habitation. Less than half of the building has been excavated. The spaces that have been excavated are for official affairs, for state affairs. These are rooms A-W (not room U), of which rooms O and J are courts, around which the other rooms are grouped.
